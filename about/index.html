About Me

Section 2.1

Omomys (fig. 2.1) was a genus of small, nocturnal ancestral primates that lived during the Eocene epoch (55-33 million years ago). Weighing less than a pound, Omomys subsisted on insects, fruit, and tree-sap. Today, Omomyid fossils are found throughout the American West, Europe, and Asia.1

1 Beard, Chris. The Hunt for the Dawn Monkey: Unearthing the Origins of Monkeys, Apes, and Humans. Berkeley: University of California Press.

Section 2.2

Lauren is a Homo sapiens sapiens (fig 2.2) living in Seattle, WA in the Holocene epoch (11,700 years ago-present). As a member of the genus Homo, she is diurnal, bipedal, and capable of cognitive thinking. Her diet consists mostly of coffee, and she has specialized hands capable of grasping, typing, and doodling. She is currently learning to code, but also quite likes paleoanthropology.
